About Me

I’m a PhD student in Machine Learning under the Cambridge-Tübingen PhD Fellowship, studying at Machine Learning Group, Computational and Biological Learning Lab, University of Cambridge and Department of Empirical Inference, Max Planck Institute for Intelligent Systems. My supervisors are José Miguel Hernández-Lobato, Bernhard Schölkopf, and Hong Ge.

I’m keen on basic research in machine learning and its scientific applications (in particular, but not limited to, molecular science). My research interest lies at the intersection of deep learning and probabilistic methods. I’m exicted about developing efficient machine learning methods for robust prediction and realistic data generation.
