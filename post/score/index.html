Diffusion Sampling and Generative Modeling

Jun 1, 2024 4 min read

Introduction

Problem Setting

  • Data distribution $$p(x)=\frac{\exp(-E(x))}{Z}$$
    • Intractable normalizing constant $$Z=\int \exp(-E(x)) dx$$
    • Tractable score function $$\nabla_x \log p(x)=-\nabla_x E(x)$$
  • Generative Modeling
    • Samples $x_1,\cdots,x_N\sim p(x)$ are available
    • The energy function $E$ is unknown and needs to be learned from data
  • Sampling
    • The energy function $E$ is given
    • Samples from $p(x)$ are not available

Diffusion

  • Gaussian convolution kernel $$p(y|x)=\mathcal{N}(y|\alpha x,\sigma^2 I)$$
  • Intractable denoising posterior and noisy marginal $$p(x|y)=\frac{p(y|x)p(x)}{p(y)}$$
  • Intractable noisy score $$\nabla_y\log p(y)=\nabla_y\log \int p(y|x)p(x)dx$$

Score Identities

Denoising Score Identity

$$\nabla_y\log p(y)=\mathbb{E}_{p(x|y)}[\nabla_y\log p(y|x)]=\int\nabla_y\log p(y|x) p(x|y)dx$$

  • Proof: By definition, we have $$ \begin{aligned} \nabla_y\log p(y) &= \frac{\int\nabla_y p(y|x)p(x)dx}{p(y)} \\ &= \int\nabla_y\log(y|x)\frac{p(y|x)p(x)}{p(y)}dx \\ &= \int\nabla_y\log p(y|x) p(x|y)dx \end{aligned} $$

Tweedie Score Identity

$$\nabla_y\log p(y)=\frac{\alpha\mathbb{E}_{p(x|y)}[ x ]-y}{\sigma^2}=\int\left(\frac{\alpha x-y}{\sigma^2}\right)p(x|y)dx$$

  • Proof: By denoising score identity, we have $$ \begin{aligned} \nabla_y\log p(y) &= \int\nabla_y\log p(y|x) p(x|y)dx \\ &= \int\nabla_y\left(-\frac{\lVert y-\alpha x\rVert^2}{2\sigma^2}\right) p(x|y)dx \\ &= \int \left(\frac{\alpha x-y}{\sigma^2}\right)p(x|y)dx \end{aligned} $$

Target Score Identity

$$\nabla_y\log p(y)=\alpha^{-1}\mathbb{E}_{p(x|y)}[\nabla_x\log p(x)]=\alpha^{-1}\int \nabla_x\log p(x) p(x|y) dx$$

  • Proof: By denoising score identity and using the following three identities $$\nabla_y \log p(y|x)=-\alpha^{-1}\nabla_x \log p(y|x)$$ $$\nabla_x \log p(y|x)=\nabla_x\log p(x|y)-\nabla_x \log p(x)$$ $$\int \nabla_x\log p(x|y)p(x|y)dx=\int \nabla_xp(x|y)dx=\nabla_x\int p(x|y)dx=0$$ we have $$ \begin{aligned} \nabla_y\log p(y) &= \int\nabla_y\log p(y|x) p(x|y)dx \\ &= -\alpha^{-1}\int\nabla_x\log p(y|x) p(x|y)dx \\ &= \alpha^{-1}\int(\nabla_x \log p(x)-\nabla_x\log p(x|y)) p(x|y)dx \\ &= \alpha^{-1}\int\nabla_x \log p(x) p(x|y)dx \\ \end{aligned} $$

Mixed Score Identity

$$\nabla_y\log p(y)=\mathbb{E}_{p(x|y)}[\alpha(x+\nabla_x\log p(x))-y]=\int (\alpha(x+\nabla_x\log p(x))-y) p(x|y) dx$$

Proof: Using a variance-preserving scheme $\sigma^2=1-\alpha^2$ and combining target score identity and denoising score identity with coefficients $\alpha^2$ and $1-\alpha^2$, we have $$ \begin{aligned} \nabla_y\log p(y) &= \int \left((\alpha\nabla_x \log p(x) + (1-\alpha^2)\nabla_y\log p(y|x)\right)p(x|y)dx \\ &= \int \left(\alpha\nabla_x \log p(x) + (1-\alpha^2)\frac{\alpha x - y}{\sigma^2}\right)p(x|y)dx \\ &= \int (\alpha(x+\nabla_x\log p(x))-y) p(x|y) dx \end{aligned} $$

Diffusion Sampling

Monte Carlo Estimator

Estimate the noisy score with Tweedie score identity using Monte Carlo $$\nabla_y \log p(y)\approx \frac{\frac{\alpha}{K}\sum_{k=1}^K x_k-y}{\sigma^2},\quad x_k\sim p(x|y)$$ Initialize the sampler for $p(x|y)$ with its mean $\mathbb{E}_{p(x|y)}[ x ]$ estimated by importance sampling $$\mathbb{E}_{p(x|y)}[ x ]\approx\frac{\sum_{l=1}^L x_l\exp(-E(x_l))}{\sum_{l=1}^L \exp(-E(x_{l}))},\quad x_l\sim q(x|y)=\mathcal{N}\left(x\left|\frac{y}{\alpha},\left(\frac{\sigma}{\alpha}\right)^2I\right)\right.$$ Proof: Using Tweedie score identity and the fact that $q(x|y)\propto p(y|x)$, we have $$ \begin{aligned} \mathbb{E}_{p(x|y)}[ x ] &= \int x p(x|y)dx \\ &= \int x \frac{p(y|x)p(x)}{p(y)}dx \\ &= \frac{\int x p(y|x)p(x)dx}{\int p(y|x)p(x)dx} \\ &= \frac{\int x \exp(-E(x))q(x|y)dx}{\int \exp(-E(x))q(x|y)dx} \\ &\approx \frac{\sum_{l=1}^L x_l\exp(-E(x_l))}{\sum_{l=1}^L \exp(-E(x_{l}))},\quad x_l\sim q(x|y) \end{aligned} $$

Importance Sampling Estimator

Estimate the noisy score with the target score identity using importance sampling $$\nabla_y \log p(y)\approx -\frac{\sum_{k=1}^K \exp(-E(x_k))\nabla_y E(x_k)}{\alpha\sum_{k=1}^K \exp(-E(x_k))},\quad x_k\sim q(x|y)=\mathcal{N}\left(x\left|\frac{y}{\alpha},\left(\frac{\sigma}{\alpha}\right)^2I\right)\right.$$ Proof: Using target score identity and the fact that $q(x|y)\propto p(y|x)$, we have $$ \begin{aligned} \nabla_y\log p(y) &= \alpha^{-1}\int \nabla_x\log p(x) p(x|y) dx \\ &= \frac{\int \nabla_x\log p(x) p(y|x)p(x) dx}{\alpha p(y)} \\ &= \frac{\int \nabla_x\log p(x) p(y|x)p(x) dx}{\alpha \int p(y|x)p(x)dx} \\ &= \frac{\int \nabla_x\log p(x) \exp(-E(x)) q(x|y) dx}{\alpha \int \exp(-E(x)) q(x|y)dx} \\ &= -\frac{\sum_{k=1}^K \exp(-E(x_k))\nabla_y E(x_k)}{\alpha\sum_{k=1}^K \exp(-E(x_k))},\quad x_k\sim q(x|y) \end{aligned} $$

Generative Modeling

Denoising Score Matching

Learn a score network $s_{\theta}(x)$ by minimizing $$L(\theta)=\mathbb{E}_{p(y)}[\lVert s_{\theta}(y) - \nabla_y \log p(y)\rVert^2]=\mathbb{E}_{p(y|x)p(x)}[\lVert s_{\theta}(y) - \nabla_y \log p(y|x)\rVert^2]+C$$ Proof: Using the denoising score identity, we have $$ \begin{aligned} \mathbb{E}_{p(y)}[\lVert s_{\theta}(y) - \nabla_y \log p(y)\rVert^2] &= \int \lVert s_{\theta}(y) - \nabla_y \log p(y)\rVert^2 p(y) dy \\ &= \int \lVert s_{\theta}(y) \rVert^2 p(y) dy - 2 \int \left<s_{\theta}(y),\nabla_y \log p(y)\right>p(y)dy + C’ \\ &= \int \lVert s_{\theta}(y) \rVert^2 p(y) dy - 2 \iint \left<s_{\theta}(y),\nabla_y\log p(y|x) \right>p(x|y)p(y)dxdy + C’ \\ &= \int \lVert s_{\theta}(y) \rVert^2 p(y) dy - 2 \iint \left<s_{\theta}(y),\nabla_y\log p(y|x) \right>p(y|x)p(x)dxdy + C’ \\ &= \iint \left( \lVert s_{\theta}(y) \rVert^2 + \lVert \nabla_y\log p(y|x) \rVert^2 - 2 \left<s_{\theta}(y),\nabla_y\log p(y|x) \right>\right)p(y|x)p(x)dxdy + C \\ &= \iint \lVert s_{\theta}(y) - \nabla_y \log p(y|x)\rVert^2 p(y|x)p(x)dxdy + C \\ &= \mathbb{E}_{p(y|x)p(x)}[\lVert s_{\theta}(y) - \nabla_y \log p(y|x)\rVert^2]+C \end{aligned} $$

Nonparametric Score Estimator

$$\nabla_y \log p(y)=\frac{\sum_{k=1}^K p(y|x_k)\nabla_y \log p(y|x_k)}{\sum_{k=1}^K p(y|x_k)},\quad x_k\sim p(x)$$ Proof: By definition of noisy score, we have $$ \begin{aligned} \nabla_y\log p(y) &= \nabla_y\log \int p(y|x)p(x)dx \\ &\approx \nabla_y\log\sum_{k=1}^K p(y|x_k),\quad x_k\sim p(x) \\ &= \frac{\sum_{k=1}^K \nabla_y p(y|x_k)}{\sum_{k=1}^K p(y|x_k)},\quad x_k\sim p(x) \\ &=\frac{\sum_{k=1}^K p(y|x_k)\nabla_y \log p(y|x_k)}{\sum_{k=1}^K p(y|x_k)},\quad x_k\sim p(x) \\ \end{aligned} $$
